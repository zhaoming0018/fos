1. Center for Nonlinear Studies
　　Center for Nonlinear Studies of Jiangsu University was founded in 1997. This center focus on various nonlinear phenomenon, especially studies the nonlinear problems appearing in the relevant disciplines by using theoretical analysis, numerical simulation and experiment. The center in recent years is engaged in more than 20 researches funded by the National Natural Science Funds and the Provincial or Ministerial Funds, obtains the first prize in progress of science and technology in Jiangsu Province, and the second prize of Shanghai science and technology progress and other prizes.

　　The center in recent years is following the international development trend of nonlinear sciences, and interested in the following directions and forms own's distinguishing features:
　(1) Dissipation of solitary wave system greatly exaggerated dissipative expansion
　(2) Dissipation solitary wave system dynamic behavior research. Long-term
　(3) Wavelet approximate inertia manifold
　(4) Infinite dimension power system of linear chaos

Deputy Director: Ding DanPing

2. International Journal of Nonlinear Science
　　International Journal of Nonlinear Science (IJNS) mainly publishes original research papers and reviews on nonlinear science and applications, including the field of mathematics, physics, biology, engineering, economics, social sciences, and other areas of science.

　　Papers can be oriented towards theory, algorithms, numerical simulations, applications or experimentation. Topics of interest include but not limited to
　(1) Solitons, coherent phenomena and complexity in nonlinear science and engineering.
　(2) Deterministic chaos, pattern formation, fractals, bifurcation, stability and singularity on fundamental or applied studies.
　(3) Analysis, modelling, simulations and control in diverse nonlinear areas.
　(4) Mathematical theories, principles and general methods in nonlinear science.

The main characteristics embodied in the study are: optical detection theory and application in biological cells, optical anomalous transmission and application.

Laser-matter Interaction Physics
　　Laser ultrasonic is an emerging interdisciplinary subject which uses laser to generate and detect ultrasonic, researching on the transmission of ultrasonic and non-destructive evaluation on material behaviors. Compared with traditional technique of piezoelectric transducer, the main advantage of Laser Ultrasonic is non-contacted detecting. It is the most promising technique of non-contacted detecting because it can be used to detect the characteristics of various complex topographies as it eliminates the influences of couplant in technique of piezoelectric transducer and it is also a detecting technique of wideband meanwhile it can use the light-wavelength as the metric to accurately measure the shift of ultrasonic. The laser ultrasonic technique can be used not only to do online non-contacted detecting on the mechanical characteristics of material but also to do research on fields of biology and medicine. It can be studied in the characteristics of material in nanometer scale if combined with scanning probe microscopy.

　　At present, this subject is mainly to consider the characteristics of material’s thermal parameter with temperature changes, using Finite Element Method to numerically simulate the transient temperature field which is excited by laser in the material, building the Finite Element Model of ultrasonic’s generation and transmission under the Thermoelastic system. It analyses the propagation characteristics of ultrasonic’s surface waviness received by far field and near field and discusses the relations between input parameters of laser and the characteristics of ultrasonic generated by laser, building the theoretical model of Laser Ultrasonic Detecting of material’s characteristics under the Thermoelastic condition and first inverting the Neural Network Approach to get the physical and geometric parameters of material. It's very important to measure the Interface Bonding Strength (tensile strength) in the research of interface science, but how to measure the interface tensile strength directly and quantitatively is still a question to scientists all over the world. It's the first time to change the style of laser loading to change the strain rate and measure the hard film's bonding strength under dynamic and static state respectively. We not only get the correct measurements of adhesion but also evaluate comprehensive mechanical properties of interface more scientifically.
